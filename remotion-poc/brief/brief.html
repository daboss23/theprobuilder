The Idea in One Page

Tools like Higgsfield, DaVinci and Artlist are two things bolted together: an AI model that generates pixels, and a layer that assembles those pixels into finished, branded videos. They charge a heavy monthly subscription for both — and unused credits expire every month.

The Pro Builder can own both layers and pay only for what it actually uses. Think of it as a kitchen:

🔥 The Oven — the AI model

Generates the raw image or video clip (e.g. Higgsfield, Veo, Kling). Rented directly by the API, billed per render. The Creative Reactor already does this today.

🔪 The Counter — Remotion

Takes the raw clip and assembles the finished ad: burned-in hook captions, TPB branding, correct aspect ratios, and multiple variants at once. Free, open-source, runs as code inside the platform. This is the new piece — proven in the attached demo.

📚 The Library — the real value

Faces, brand assets, proven hook templates and past winners. This is where lasting value lives — and it maps directly onto the Reactor's existing Knowledge Vault.

How It Works — End to End

A single brief flows through four stages and comes out as a finished, on-brand ad — no manual editing, no subscription tool.

1
Knowledge Vault
Every winning asset, framework and member win is embedded and made searchable (Voyage + pgvector).
2
Campaign Reactor (the agent)
A Claude agent reasons over what has already worked and writes the angle, hook, body and CTA — grounded in real evidence.
3
AI Oven
Generates the supporting image and video clip from the agent's creative direction.
4
Remotion Counter
Layers the hook caption + TPB branding over the clip, cuts it to 9:16 / 1:1 / 16:9, and exports finished variants.

What it can produce

  • Video: multi-scene ads, captioned reels, product demos, faceless content, B-roll packs, data/motion graphics.
  • Images: the same engine exports any single frame as a PNG — ad stills, thumbnails, carousel slides.
  • Variants: one brief → a pack of versions automatically (different hooks, sizes, looks).
